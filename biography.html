Biography

I am a postdoctoral research associate at the University of Southern California (USC) working with Salman Avestimehr. My research interests are in the areas of machine learning, distributed computation, wireless communications, networks, and information theory. My current research focus is on secure, trustworthy, and verifiable federated learning, privacy-preserving machine learning, responsible data economics and valuation for machine learning and analytics, blockchain systems, and timely information exchange in distributed systems. In my research, I utilize tools and techniques from optimization, machine learning, statistics, applied cryptography, information and coding theory.

I received my Ph.D. degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Maryland (UMD), College Park, MD, USA in 2021 under the supervision of Sennur Ulukus. During my doctoral studies, I worked on timely information delivery in large networks, distributed computation and machine learning systems. Prior to joining UMD, I received my B.S. degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from Bilkent University, Turkey in 2016.

I have been fortunate to collaborate with Deniz Gunduz (Imperial College London), Songze Li (The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology), Hessam Mahdavifar (University of Michigan), Yupeng Zhang (Texas A&M University), and Alkan Soysal (Virgina Tech) during my Ph.D. and postdoc.
